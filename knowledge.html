01 - Crypto Basics

What is a Blockchain?

Think of a blockchain as a shared public notebook that thousands of computers maintain simultaneously. Once confirmed, records cannot be changed.

Key properties

  • Decentralised - no single authority controls it.
  • Transparent - transactions are publicly verifiable.
  • Immutable - confirmed records are permanent.
  • Trustless - rules are enforced by smart contracts.
Why this matters for $TSUN: contract status and LP lock can be independently verified on-chain.

05 - Crypto Mechanics

How a DEX & Swaps Work

A DEX allows wallet-to-wallet trades via smart contracts without centralized custody.

Liquidity Pools

Trades execute against pools, not order books. Pool ratio sets market price.

Price Impact & Slippage

Large trades in smaller pools move price more. Slippage settings protect execution limits.

Gas Fees on TON

Keep a small TON balance for fees to avoid failed transactions.
